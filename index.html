WELCOME TO QUANTUM ROBOTICS
// Mission Brief

Quantum Robotics Division welcomes high school students under 19, particularly those new to the VEX Robotics Competition. A nurturing environment built to develop foundational robotics skills.

// 01 · Mission Briefing

About Us

C-1 · Key Features
Features

This division provides a comprehensive introduction to robotics, with an emphasis on mentor-led training and development.

Students are introduced to essential robotics technologies and team collaboration practices — building both technical ability and interpersonal skills.

Core Module
C-2 · Educational Outcomes
Skills Gained

Participants will gain:

  • Mechanical & electronic engineering, programming, sensor technology, and CAD
  • Engineering design process and effective documentation practices
  • Teamwork, communication, and problem-solving
Training
// 02 · Operations Timeline

Schedule

01
Phase One
Early Season
June – August
Pre-season preparation, initial design sprints, and team formation. Students establish engineering foundations.
02
Phase Two
Regular Season
September – March
Full competition season. Teams meet twice weekly — design, build, programming, and engineering notebook maintenance.
03
Phase Three
Post Season
March – May
Reflection, documentation, and preparation for the next cycle. Knowledge transfer to incoming members.
// 03 · Mentorship
Support &
Resources

Every team is paired with dedicated adult mentors who provide guidance throughout the season. Students gain access to structured seminars, hands-on clinics, and a supportive network designed to accelerate learning at every level.

01
Dedicated Mentors
Each team is assigned adult mentors with engineering and robotics expertise throughout the entire season.
02
Structured Seminars
Regular sessions covering design methodology, programming fundamentals, and competition strategy.
03
Hands-On Clinics
Focused build and programming clinics to reinforce skills and tackle specific technical challenges.
